Guide Questions 3: What is Prototyping?

What is Prototyping?

image credit:

Prototyping is a preliminary and sometimes physical simulation of a proposed digital solution. It is an experimental process where teams aim to capture design concepts and test them with users (Prototyping, n.d.). Prototypes give users the opportunity to interact with the figurative digital solution; get a feel for the actual app. The material visualization gives a better idea of how tentative features will work.

What is the difference between low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes?

Fidelity refers to the detail and functionality included in the prototype (Prototyping, n.d.).

image credit: Liu [2]

Low-fidelity prototypes are very rough drafts of the app. It focuses on layout and showcasing main features. This prototype utilizes minimal labelling, making use of simple shapes as placeholders.

This prototype is ideal for quick use and feedback. However, its portrayal of the actual features may be too vague for the users to comprehend.

High-fidelity prototypes on the other hand, are highly detailed visualizations. The features are well-defined, almost close to if the solution has been built. Users can clearly simulate interactions with this prototype.

image credit: Caubang, D. et al [1]

This prototype is ideal for accurate simulation and feedback. Drawbacks to this approach is that it is time consuming to produce and the designer may be biased against changing the prototype.

Why is prototyping important?

image credit:

The importance of prototyping lies in the finals stages of ux design. Before implementing the actual digital solution, it is essential to test key features. The design team aims to solve users' pain points and have to confirm if the features to be implemented will serve their functions. Although prototyping is still partly iterative, it saves work on coding in the long run. There would be less need to pull unnecessarily changes. Overall, prototyping is always committed to users' needs in mind, particularly with an eye for user flow (Prototyping, n.d.).
